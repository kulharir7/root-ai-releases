Automate Your Browser with AI —
No Coding Required

Feb 22, 2026 · 6 min read · By Ravindra Kumar

You do it every day. Open Chrome. Go to a website. Click a button. Fill a form. Copy some data. Paste it somewhere else. Repeat. Over and over and over.

What if you could just tell your computer what you want from the browser, and it does it for you? No Selenium scripts. No Puppeteer code. No browser extensions to configure. Just plain English.

That's browser automation with Korvus.

How It Works

Korvus connects to your Chrome browser and can control it programmatically. But unlike traditional automation tools, you don't write code — you describe what you want in natural language.

Korvus then:

  1. Opens Chrome (or connects to your existing session)
  2. Takes a snapshot of the page (reads the DOM structure)
  3. Understands the UI (identifies buttons, forms, links, text)
  4. Performs actions (click, type, scroll, screenshot, extract data)
  5. Reports results back to you

Real Examples

Example 1: Research
"Go to Amazon India, search for wireless earbuds under ₹2000, and list the top 5 results with their prices and ratings"

Korvus opens Amazon, types the search, applies the price filter, reads the product cards, and gives you a clean summary.

Example 2: Form Filling
"Open the job application form at [URL], fill in my name as Ravindra Kumar, email as my@email.com, and upload my resume from Documents/resume.pdf"

Korvus navigates to the form, identifies each field, fills in the data, finds the file upload button, and attaches your resume.

Example 3: Data Extraction
"Go to this competitor's pricing page, extract all plan names and prices, and save them as a CSV file"

Korvus visits the page, identifies the pricing table, extracts the data, formats it, and creates a CSV file on your computer.

Example 4: Social Media
"Open my GitHub profile, take a screenshot, and check if I have any new notifications"

Korvus opens GitHub, takes a screenshot for your records, checks the notification bell, and summarizes any new activity.

Example 5: Monitoring
"Check if the product at [URL] is back in stock. If yes, tell me the price."

Korvus visits the page, checks the stock status, and reports back. Set this up as a scheduled task and get notified via Telegram when it's available.

Traditional Automation vs AI Automation

The Old Way (Selenium/Puppeteer)

from selenium import webdriver
from selenium.webdriver.common.by import By

driver = webdriver.Chrome()
driver.get("https://amazon.in")
search = driver.find_element(By.ID, "twotabsearchtextbox")
search.send_keys("wireless earbuds")
search.submit()
# ... 50 more lines of brittle selectors
# Breaks when Amazon updates their HTML

Problems: You need to know programming. Selectors break when the website updates. Error handling is complex. Maintaining scripts is a nightmare.

The AI Way (Korvus)

"Search Amazon for wireless earbuds and show me the top 5 results"

Benefits: No coding. No selectors. Doesn't break when the website updates (AI understands the page semantically). Works on any website without custom scripts.

Traditional browser automation is like giving your computer a treasure map with exact coordinates. AI automation is like telling your computer "find the treasure" and it figures out the path itself.

What You Can Automate

  • Data collection — Extract prices, reviews, stats from any website
  • Form filling — Applications, surveys, registrations
  • Screenshots — Capture pages, specific sections, or full-page shots
  • Navigation — Open specific pages, click through menus
  • Login & interact — Log into sites and perform authenticated actions
  • Monitoring — Check for changes, stock availability, price drops
  • Testing — Test your own websites and web apps

Tips for Best Results

  1. Be specific: "Search Amazon for Sony WH-1000XM5" works better than "find headphones"
  2. Break complex tasks: Instead of one huge command, use 2-3 smaller steps
  3. Use Screen Vision: Press Ctrl+Shift+S to capture what you see and ask AI about it
  4. Combine with file system: "Scrape data from this page AND save it as CSV" — Korvus handles both

Privacy Note

Korvus controls your local Chrome browser. It doesn't use any remote browser or proxy. The browser runs on your machine, visible on your screen. You can watch everything happening in real-time.

No browser data, cookies, or sessions are sent anywhere. Your logins stay local.
